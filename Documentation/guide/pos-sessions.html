POS Sessions ​

The POS Sessions module tracks all activity related to registers, including when a cashier opens or closes a session, cash handling, and sales summaries. Sessions provide accountability and transparency for all cash and register operations.

Index Page ​

The index page lists all sessions created for each register with details such as branch, register name, cashier, status, opening float, declared cash, sales totals, and timestamps.

Filters ​

  • Branch – Filter sessions by branch.
  • POS Register – Show sessions for a specific register.
  • Status – Filter sessions by Open or Closed.
  • From / To – Filter sessions by creation or closing date range.

Actions ​

  • Open Session – Start a new session for a register.
  • Row Actions:
    • Show Session – View full session details and summaries.
    • View Cash Movements – Display all cash-in and cash-out operations tied to this session.
    • Close Session – Available only if the session is still Open.

Open Session ​

When opening a session, the cashier must define initial session details:

AttributeRequiredNotes
Branch✅ YesRequired only if the user is not assigned to a specific branch.
POS Register✅ YesSelect which register is being opened.
Opening Float❌ OptionalStarting cash in the drawer. Useful for reconciliation at closing.
Notes❌ OptionalAdd context or remarks for the session.

Once confirmed, the session becomes active (Open), and all orders, sales, and cash movements during this time are logged under it.

Close Session ​

Closing a session finalizes all activities and requires reconciliation:

AttributeRequiredNotes
Declared Cash✅ YesThe amount of cash physically counted by the cashier at the end of the session.

The system automatically compares Declared Cash with System Cash Sales and calculates any cash over/short discrepancies. After closing, no further transactions can be assigned to this session.

Show Session (Details Page) ​

The Show Session page provides a full breakdown of the session’s activity:

Header Information ​

  • Branch & POS Register – Where the session was opened.
  • Status – Open or Closed.
  • Opened By / Closed By – User accounts who opened and closed the session.
  • Opened At / Closed At – Timestamps for session duration.

Financial Details ​

  • Opening Float – Initial cash in the drawer.
  • Declared Cash – Cash counted at closing.
  • System Cash Sales – Expected cash based on recorded orders.
  • System Card Sales – Card payments processed in this session.
  • System Other Sales – Any other payment methods (wallets, vouchers, etc.).
  • Total Sales – Overall sales processed during the session.
  • Total Refunds – Refunds issued during the session.
  • Orders Count – Number of orders processed.
  • Cash Over/Short – Difference between declared cash and system-recorded cash sales.

Related Data ​

  • Orders – Orders handled in this session.
  • Cash Movements – Manual cash in/out operations (e.g., petty cash, safe drops).
  • Notes – Any additional information provided by the cashier.

Notes ​

  • Sessions ensure register accountability by tying all activity to a cashier and time frame.
  • A register can only have one active (Open) session at a time.
  • Closing sessions is mandatory before opening a new one for the same register.
  • Managers can review sessions to detect discrepancies, track refunds, and audit cash handling.
